F1 Standard Ratios (per 1 liter)

Classic Black Tea
Tea: 2 bags (or 4g loose) · Sugar: 50-70g white · Starter: 100-150ml
The standard. Full-bodied, forgiving, fastest fermentation. Your current base.
Green Tea
Tea: 2 bags (or 3g loose) · Sugar: 50-60g · Starter: 100-150ml
Lighter, more delicate. Slightly slower fermentation. Shorter steep (2-3 min) to avoid bitterness.
Oolong Tea
Tea: 3g loose (or 2 bags) · Sugar: 50-60g · Starter: 100-150ml
Best of both worlds — complexity of black, lightness of green. Great for floral F2 flavors.
White Tea
Tea: 3-4g loose (or 2 bags) · Sugar: 50-60g · Starter: 120-150ml
Very mild, subtle. Slowest fermentation. Use more starter to compensate. Pairs with fruit F2.

Taming Strong Kombucha

Your kombucha comes out strong — here are ways to dial it back:
  • Shorter F1: Pull 2-3 days earlier than usual. Check pH — aim for the sweet spot range instead of going past the sour threshold
  • Less tea: Try reducing from 2 bags/L to 1.5 bags/L
  • More sugar: Try 70g/L instead of 50g. More residual sweetness balances tartness
  • More starter isn't always better: Over 15% starter makes it tart faster. Try 10% (100ml per liter)
  • Temperature: Cooler fermentation (18-22°C) produces milder, smoother kombucha vs hot/fast
  • Green or oolong base: Naturally milder than black tea

F2 Flavoring Recipes (per 500ml bottle)

Ginger + Turmeric Your Recipe
15-20g fresh ginger (sliced) + 5-10g fresh turmeric (or 1/2 tsp powder)
Anti-inflammatory powerhouse. Add 1 tsp sugar for carbonation. 2-4 days F2. Tip: grate for stronger flavor, slice for milder.
Dry Hopped Your Recipe
3-5g whole hops (Cascade, Citra, or Galaxy work well)
IPA-style kombucha. Add hops directly. 2-3 days F2 max or it gets grassy. No extra sugar needed — hops don't add carbonation but kombucha should have enough residual sugar. Strain before drinking.
Popular Recipes to Try
Ginger + Lemon
15g fresh ginger (grated) + juice of 1/4 lemon + 1 tsp sugar
Bright and zesty. Classic crowd-pleaser. The lemon juice adds acidity so check carbonation at day 2.
Mixed Berry
30-40g mashed berries (blueberry, raspberry, strawberry)
Beautiful color, fruity sweetness. Fruit sugars drive strong carbonation — burp bottles daily! 2-3 days F2.
Mango + Habanero
30g mango (diced) + 1 thin slice habanero (seeds removed)
Sweet heat. Start with less pepper — heat intensifies during F2. 2-3 days.
Apple + Cinnamon
30g apple (diced or juiced) + 1/2 cinnamon stick
Autumn vibes. Apple juice drives good carbonation. Cinnamon adds warmth without sugar. 3-4 days F2.
Lavender + Lemon
1 tsp dried culinary lavender + juice of 1/4 lemon + 1 tsp honey
Floral and refreshing. Use food-grade lavender only. Pairs beautifully with green or oolong base.
Yuzu + Shiso
2 tbsp yuzu juice + 3-4 fresh shiso leaves
Japanese-inspired. Yuzu adds bright citrus, shiso adds herbal complexity. Easy to find in Tokyo. 2-3 days F2.
Ume (Plum) + Ginger
1 umeboshi (pitted, mashed) or 2 tbsp ume syrup + 10g fresh ginger
Salty-sour-sweet. Uniquely Japanese. The salt from umeboshi adds interesting depth. 2-3 days F2.
Passionfruit
Pulp of 1 fresh passionfruit (seeds and all)
Tropical, aromatic, gorgeous. Seeds are fine to leave in. Very active carbonation — burp daily. 2 days F2 is usually enough.
Elderflower
2 tbsp elderflower cordial or 1 tbsp dried elderflower
Delicate, floral, champagne-like. Best with white or green tea base. Light carbonation. 3-4 days F2.
Cold Brew Coffee
60ml cold brew concentrate (unsweetened)
Coffee kombucha! Rich and complex. Caffeine boost. Best with black tea base. 2 days F2. Don't over-ferment or it gets bitter.

pH Reference Guide

Track pH to know exactly when your kombucha is ready:
How to measure pH: Use pH test strips (pH 0-6 range, ~¥500-1000 on Amazon Japan) or a digital pH meter (~¥2000-3000). Dip strip in a small sample, compare color to the chart. Test every 2-3 days during F1.
